Vladikavkaz

Welcome to a small, but very cozy town at the Caucasus Mountains foothills.

A town shrouded in the patina of time, a town that is ready to embrace anyone who appreciates it's modest beauty.

Every season is the best time of the year to visit!

From spring to autumn the landscape is full of bright colors and the weather is always in this perfect range of comfortable warmth.

In the mountains, however, the weather is unpredictable and constantly changing even in summer.

As soon as you arrive, immediately go out for a walk along Mira Avenue and all adjacent streets. You will be pleasantly surprised by a variety of eclectic mansions, each one of which has a story more interesting than any novel. Various workshops, ateliers, restaurants, museums and shops are located here.

Vladikavkaz seems to breathe history: in a beautiful dance, the aristocratic spirit of pre-revolutionary Russia and the Caucasian culture have merged in it.

Hear the heavy trills of old trams?

It's the only transport allowed on this pedestrian street. A historic tram runs around Vladikavkaz, and on holidays it is served with tea.

Don't miss the chance to ride! You can take a tour and learn about the history of the town while riding comfortably through all the major streets.

Industrialists and artisans, officials and lawyers settled in town center with high hopes for this booming town. These bourgeois houses hide many stories, secrets and tragedies.

A small truck can still squeeze through the carriage gates, and schoolkids roll down the time-polished stone stairs. There are pharmacies here that have always been pharmacies, and there are banks that have always been banks.

You can escape from the buzzy srteets in the cool shade of centuries-old trees in the Central Park. Located at the very heart of the town, it is a place for reflection and childhood memories.

National sculptures, a small open-air cinema stage, spots for all kinds of cultural and sport activities. Bridges, fontains, all touched by time. If there is a time machine, it's definitely parked here.

The location looks especially magical at sunset, when the last sun rays barely illuminate the swans swimming in the pond.

Ossetians have an incredible cult of food, so a good barbecue and pies out in the mountains is the most popular way to spend the weekend here.

In this relatively small area, local pagan religion coexists with all the monotheistic confessions. Because of the already existing concept of a single God, they never fully took over, instead various practices only partially merged into local tradition.

One of the main symbols of the city is the Sunni Mosque in a peculiar egyptian style. It is very reminiscent of such mosques that can be seen around Cairo.

This project was commisioned by a wealthy industrialist for his beloved wife in 1900s. Usually tourists are allowed to go inside when the prayer service isn't being held.

The views of mountains will tempt you from everywhere, and sooner or later you will find yourself heading towards them. And it doesn't take long, in just half an hour from the town center you can find yourself on a winding mountain road, surrounded by dangerous cliffs and stormy rivers.

Be careful with all those glaciers, waterfalls and meadows though, there is a chance you will want to sell all your posessions and settle here.

Deep in the Tsey Gorge there is another unique sanctuary of a great sacred and scientific significance - Rekom.

Mountains are very special for Ossetians, that's why there are so many holy places here. In ossetian understanding, the temple is not limited to one building, all the surrounding nature, even the entire gorge can be sacred.

Eastern chroniclers wrote that “it was extremely difficult to get there, due to the height, which was so great that the eye of the beholder was clouded, and the hat fell off the head.”

The builders secured the building as much as possible from the invasion of enemies. Here people not only defended themselves from enemies, but also lived.

For outdoor activities you can always turn to guides. Route options are extensive, and even the most demanding tourist will find something to their liking.

Vladikavkaz has changed for the better lately, which is confirmed by the crowds of tourists who can recently be found walking along the winding streets.

The city is not going to lose momentum - it has become very comfortable for domestic and international tourism, the historical district has been actively restored, and many recreational places have appeared on the map.

For a short weekend, Vladikavkaz is an ideal option!

In spring you can catch cherry blossoms, the fragrance of alpine meadows, glades with irises. Autumn will show you the waterfalls and springs in their best. Even in winter there is no northern cold in these latitudes, so it's ideal time for inexperienced skiing or extreme mountain climbing.

An old boulevard stretches across the entire central part of the Avenue. For the locals walking along Mira Avenue means both looking at people and showing yourself, and at evenings everyone is here.

An important protagonist of the evening stroll is the radio "Town". Jazz, blues, symphonies, as well as news and announcements from the last century float over the town.

Mira Avenue also offers one of the best views of mesa mountain, associated with a very beautiful and tragic legend of a young woman who sacrificed her life for her people, and now the shape of her dead body is towering over the town.

But the real soul of Vladikavkaz is further, in the small red-brick houses with front gardens, carefully planted rose bushes at the entrance, large fruit trees, planted a hundred years ago by someone's great-grandfather.

These cozy cramped streets with tiny family houses awaken nostalgia for something long lost.

Behind the park you will find yourself on the bank of Terek river. You can go through the whole town, walking along the bank of this stormy wayward river. Cool wind and loud waterfalls will help to cut yourself off from the outside world and to get lost in your own thoughts.

Food tradition reflects local religious beliefs, so the thin pies with different fillings are usually served three at a time, as a symbol of the trinity of the universe. In order to blend with the locals you better eat with your bare hands.

Mountains are filled with ancient sanctuaries and sacred natural areas, while various temples of other confessions are built in the urban area. Some of those are new and fancy, others remember those times when Vladikavkaz was just a fortress.

Another unusual for the south of Russia building is a Lutheran church built at the request of the German community in 1861. Modest building in gothic style, which now houses the Philharmonic organization, is an object of cultural heritage and is one of the most important centers of musical life in the region.

Some places can be easily reached by car, others are ideal for solitude. Dizzying beauty is the only common thing they all share.

At the entrance to the Alagir Gorge, guests are greeted by a sculpture of the guardian of travelers.

Everyone who steps outside of the house is considered a traveler in Ossetia, and people still turn to Uastirdzhi with a request that they return home safe and sound, especially upon entering the treacherous mountain roads.

This mysterious monument is the only sanctuary in Caucasus region built of logs, and a large amount of archaeological material was gathered here. Locals treat it with deep reverence.

A powerful fortification of Dzivgis is literally stuck to the rocks.

It has repeatedly saved the Ossetians during the raids of the conquerors. The fortress consists of several towers, which are attached to natural caves.

There is a late medieval Ossetian necropolis outside the village of Dargavs called the "City of the Dead". This architectural and ethnographic complex dates back to the 14th century and is perfectly preserved.

It is believed it was used during plague epidemic, when infected people went to the crypts, where they spent the last days of their lives.

If you are not that much into hiking, you can still experience the fresh mountain air, incredible food and the view of the milky way above your head from the comfort of the hotels.
